Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Presently

Is this just another productivity app?
Is this just another productivity app?
Nope. It's the opposite. Presently isn't here to optimize you — it's here to accompany you. Especially on the days when you feel foggy, frozen, or like your brain hit a wall and bounced.
Do I have to talk or keep my camera on?
Do I have to talk or keep my camera on?
Not at all. Most rooms are silent by default. Mic and camera are there if you want them. No forced small talk. No "quick intros." Just focus and go.
Are there fixed session times?
Are there fixed session times?
Nope again. Come in for five minutes or five hours — it's drop-in, drop-out. No countdown clocks yelling at you.
Do I have to check in or say what I'm working on?
Do I have to check in or say what I'm working on?
Only if it helps you. You can share a task, set an intention, or just lurk quietly. No social energy required.
How much does it cost?
How much does it cost?
Early access is free. Long-term, we're planning. . . still planning.
Do you have printable tools or offline options?
Do you have printable tools or offline options?
Yes! We're adding simple, paper-friendly templates for people who can't look at another screen - or just need to scribble their way out of executive dysfunction.

You'll get our free printable Focus Kit (includes a weekly planner, one-win-per-day tracker, and work ritual guide) as a thank-you when you join the waitlist ✨
Is this for people with ADHD only?
Is this for people with ADHD only?
Nope. While it's designed with ADHD and neurodivergent users in mind, presently is for anyone who feels disconnected, overwhelmed, or just needs a gentle way to start again.
Is there an app?
Is there an app?
Not yet. We're starting browser-based to keep it simple and light. An app might come later, but we're focused on doing one thing well first.
What if I join and still don't get anything done?
What if I join and still don't get anything done?
Then you showed up anyway — and that counts. presently isn't here to measure your output. It's here to offer space. You can start again tomorrow (or in ten minutes — we're not the boss of you).
